Where Did Our Moon Come From?

The best theory that scientists have came up with on how our one and only moon formed and came to be started long ago. About 4.5 Billion years ago it is estimated that a large "Mars sized object" collided with the earth liquifiing the surface of each celestial body. A large chunk of molten rock was sent into space and a few thousand years later gravity pulled a large clump of the depris and formed our moon. The rest of the particles and larger rock chunks fell back to the earth or got absorbed into this new body of rock called our Moon.

Why Does The Near Side Look So Much Different Than The Far Side

If you take a picture of the near side and the far side of the moon and put them next to eachother they look completly different. On the side that faces us there are huge dark spots called seas but on the far side barley any, why is this? Its because of one major event that scientists belive happened about 3.8 Billion years ago. Its thought that due to the near side having such a thin crust compared to the far side, when a series of large astroids smashed into the moon it filled certain areas of the moon with lava and heavy volcanic activity creating these seas of lava. As time passed these pools of lava harded creating the seas we know and lable today.
